How Public Institutions Can Lawfully Recover Outstanding Revenues Without Creating Legal Exposure.

A strategic framework for identifying, validating, and pursuing public revenue claims through negotiation, litigation, and enforcement — while managing the legal and institutional risks that arise when government entities pursue recovery.

Public institutions across Nigeria hold billions in outstanding revenues — from unpaid statutory levies and contractual debts to disputed receivables and enforcement backlogs. Many of these claims are valid, documented, and recoverable. Yet they remain uncollected, often for years.

The reasons are familiar: institutional reluctance to litigate without a clear mandate, uncertainty about the legal basis for recovery, fear of creating political or legal exposure, and a shortage of senior legal counsel with the credibility and authority to lead these matters through negotiation, litigation, and enforcement.

The strategic framework

Effective public revenue recovery requires a structured, phased approach. It begins with identification and validation — a forensic review of outstanding claims to determine which are legally sound, which require further documentation, and which carry risks that need to be managed before any demand is made.

Once claims are validated, the recovery strategy must be tailored to the nature of the debtor, the size and complexity of the claim, and the institutional context. Some matters are best resolved through structured negotiation and settlement. Others require litigation, arbitration, or enforcement proceedings — and the credibility to pursue them to conclusion.

“The right recovery strategy is the one that delivers a practical outcome — not just a legal argument.

Managing legal exposure

Public institutions face unique risks when pursuing recovery. Claims must be properly authorised, processes must comply with public procurement and fiscal responsibility frameworks, and recovery actions must be defensible against challenge. A poorly structured recovery effort can create more legal exposure than it resolves.

The role of senior counsel

High-value public revenue matters require more than legal drafting. They require institutional credibility, courtroom authority, regulatory understanding, and the judgment to know when to negotiate, when to litigate, and when to enforce. This is the platform that 24 Law Chambers provides.
